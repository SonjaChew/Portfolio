Asha India

Non-Profit Website Redesign
Role: UX/UI Designer
Team: Sonja Chew, Deepa Bharadwaj, Lydia Huang, & Melissa Mokhtari
Duration: 3 weeks




Who is Asha India?

Asha India is a nonprofit organization dedicated to saving lives of the impoverished living in the slums of Delhi, India. Asha-India.org was created in order to allow users a way to donate and provide updates on how the different programs of Asha are utilizing their monetary benefaction and to show the changed lives of beneficiaries.



The Problem


The original Asha India website uses a structured blogging framework, which constrains the site from optimizing on user engagement and donations. Also, with increased access to mobile technology in Delhi slums over the upcoming years, we see an opportunity to expand the website's target users to both donors and beneficiaries of the organization.

Our Solution


We proposed to redesign the layout of key web pages identified through intensive user testing performing important tasks. Based on feedback, we restructured the information architecture, conducted responsive and interactive design testing, and designed two separate experiences for the donors and the beneficiaries.




User Research

User Personas


Because we had two target audiences for this website redesign, we created two user personas to represent them. One persona represents a donor and the other represents a beneficiary.


Affinity Mapping


After fleshing out our User Personas and their goals, we performed the Affinity Mapping exercise to further understand key goals and pain points of our users.


Our Redesign Goals


Looking at the original website, there is so much chaos and disorganization of information. We wanted to speak to stake-holders as a way of hearing exactly what goals they had in mind for Asha-India.org. Unfortunately, no one responded to our efforts to make contact, so we went ahead with the redesign based off of the mission statement on Asha-India.org.

User Flows


We considered what user flows our user personas might take and designed two paths, one for each type of user.




Information Architecture

Evaluating the Existing Site


As a team, we conducted a page-by-page analysis of the UI of Asha-India.org. We selected pages to analyze based on their importance to our selected User Task Flows.


Site Mapping


We prioritized the main user flows based on our personas, and mapped out the existing website accordingly. We then reordered and reorganized the elements in order to make the website more intuitive, and make it easier for users to perform key tasks quickly and efficiently.

High Fidelity Prototypes


We initially adopted a very illustrative and colorful approach to the UI redesign, aiming for a friendly, interactive, personalized feel and touch to the desktop version that catered primarily to the Donor. But realized that it could be distracting as the website is very content heavy. The design we settled on was one utilized white space as well as incorporated many images and a more simple color theme in order to invoke as compassion to users.
